Scott Galloway has one question underneath every question he asks: Does the math work? Not the dream. Not the vision deck. The math.

Market size. Demographic tailwinds. Margin profile. Differentiation. Distribution. Capital efficiency. Narrative. Founder-market fit. Eight dimensions. Each one is a door. Most ideas don't make it through all eight.

"The biggest thing we get wrong about success is thinking it has something to do with the quality of the idea. The world is littered with great ideas. What matters is whether you have the unfair advantages to execute at scale." — Scott Galloway

This tool runs your idea through the framework. Answer honestly. The score tells you where you stand — and more importantly, where the holes are.

Question 1 of 8
Question 01
Dimension: Market Size
How large is the market you're addressing?
Galloway's rule: if the market isn't at least $10B, the upside can't justify the risk. He's not interested in winning a small pond.

Question 02
Dimension: Demographic Tailwind
Which demographic wave does your idea ride?
Demographics are destiny. The strongest ideas align with a population that's growing, aging, or gaining economic power — not declining.
Prof G Would Say
Question 03
Dimension: Differentiation
What's your actual moat?
"First mover" is not a moat. "Better product" is not a moat. A moat is something structural that makes it hard for the next person to duplicate what you've built.
Prof G Would Say
Question 04
Dimension: Margin Profile
What's the gross margin ceiling of this business?
Margin is the oxygen. Software is 80%+. Media can be 60-70%. Physical products rarely clear 40%. Galloway builds businesses where every additional dollar of revenue costs almost nothing to produce.
Prof G Would Say
Question 05
Dimension: Distribution
Do you have unfair access to customers?
Distribution beats product. The best idea without a distribution channel is a manuscript in a drawer. The question isn't how good the product is — it's whether you can get it in front of people.
Prof G Would Say
Question 06
Dimension: Capital Efficiency
How much capital does it take to reach meaningful revenue?
Galloway respects capital efficiency — especially when the founder isn't coming from wealth. The less capital required to validate the model, the more power stays with the creator.
Prof G Would Say
Question 07
Dimension: Narrative
Can you say why this matters in one sentence — without buzzwords?
Not the elevator pitch. Not the mission statement. One honest sentence that explains who is winning, who is losing, and why your idea changes that.
Prof G Would Say
Question 08
Dimension: Founder–Market Fit
Why are you the one who builds this?
Galloway's last question is always the hardest one. Great markets with the wrong founder is still a bad bet. Founder-market fit is the X factor the spreadsheet can't capture.
